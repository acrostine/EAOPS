Deviation and exception management ()
A solution design review feedback is received, identifying a non-conformance that requires exception consideration, which triggers the deviation and exception management process. This process involves several key activities, including receiving the deviation request, assessing the design review findings, evaluating the associated risks and business case, recording the disposition decision, and either closing the case or escalating it as necessary. Stakeholders such as the architecture lead, review board, and project team are involved throughout the lifecycle to ensure that all perspectives are considered in the decision-making process. The existence of this process is critical for maintaining compliance with established architectural principles and ensuring that any deviations are managed effectively. Without this process, there could be significant risks to architectural integrity, leading to potential misalignment with stakeholder needs and standards.
