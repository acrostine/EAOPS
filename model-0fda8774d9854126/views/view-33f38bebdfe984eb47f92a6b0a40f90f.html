A project request is submitted by a delivery team requiring an engagement assessment from the Enterprise Architecture (EA) team. The process of project intake and architecture scoping involves assessing the relevance of the project to architectural standards, defining the scope and engagement level, identifying applicable architecture patterns, and issuing the necessary outputs for further analysis and pattern provision. Stakeholders such as the project team, architecture lead, and EA practitioner collaborate throughout this lifecycle to ensure that the project aligns with established architectural principles and frameworks. The process exists to ensure that projects are effectively aligned with architectural standards, providing clarity and direction for the delivery teams. Without this structured approach, projects may deviate from architectural guidelines, leading to inefficiencies and potential misalignment with organizational goals.
