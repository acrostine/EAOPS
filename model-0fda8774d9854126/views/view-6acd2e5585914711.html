Release authorisation and publication - Context ()
The release authorisation and publication process is initiated when approved model changes are received from review and approval coordination. This process encompasses the verification of authorisation conditions, the assembly of the release package, and the issuance of release approval documentation, ensuring that all stakeholders, including the architecture lead, review board, and EA practitioners, are engaged throughout its lifecycle. It exists to deliver value by ensuring that releases are properly governed and documented; without this process, the quality and compliance of releases would degrade, ultimately impacting the architecture publishing output received by quality assurance. <!-- SPEC --> **Lifecycle phases** - receive approved model changes: the process begins with the receipt of model changes that have been approved by the review board. - verify authorisation conditions: the team checks that all necessary authorisation conditions are met before proceeding. - assemble release package: the release package is compiled, including all relevant documents and information. - issue release approval documentation: the final release approval documentation is generated and prepared for distribution. - confirm publication handoff: the process concludes with the confirmation that the release has been handed off for publication quality assurance. **Information objects** - release package: produced by the assembly phase, arrives at the issue phase, passed to the confirm publication handoff. - architecture model: produced by the approved model changes, arrives at the verify authorisation conditions phase, passed to the assemble release package. - architecture decision: produced by the review board, arrives at the verify authorisation conditions phase, passed to the assemble release package. - compliance report: produced by the review board, arrives at the verify authorisation conditions phase, passed to the assemble release package. - release approval documentation: produced by the issue phase, arrives at the confirm publication handoff phase, passed to publication quality assurance.
