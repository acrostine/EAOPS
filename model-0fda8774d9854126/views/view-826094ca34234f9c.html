Solution design review - Context ()
The solution design review process is initiated when a project team submits a solution design for architecture review. This process results in the delivery of solution design review feedback to the deviation and exception management team. The process encompasses several key activities, including receiving the review request, applying architecture principles, identifying conformance gaps, formulating review feedback, and issuing findings to deviation management. Stakeholders involved in this process include the architecture lead, EA practitioner, and the project team, all of whom play critical roles in ensuring that the solution aligns with established architectural standards throughout its lifecycle. This process exists to ensure that all solution designs adhere to the defined architecture principles, thereby maintaining compliance and mitigating risks associated with deviations. Without this review, the organization may face challenges in upholding architectural integrity, leading to potential inefficiencies and inconsistencies in project outcomes.
