Capability increment scoping ()
The capability increment scoping process is initiated when an initiative sequencing decision is received from investment coherence and portfolio sequencing. This process involves breaking down the initiative into specific model changes, assessing their feasibility and effort, and ultimately drafting a scoped increment definition that will guide the subsequent model development and semantic validation phases. Key stakeholders in this process include the architecture lead, EA practitioner, and review board, who collaborate to ensure that the scoped increment aligns with governance requirements and strategic objectives. The process encompasses several lifecycle phases, beginning with the receipt of the sequencing decision, followed by the decomposition of the initiative into model changes. It then moves to assessing the feasibility and effort required for these changes, drafting the scoped increment definition, and finally confirming and publishing the definition to development. Each phase is critical for ensuring that the release cycle is well-governed and that the changes proposed are achievable within the given constraints. This process exists to deliver value by ensuring that the release cycle is effectively managed and that increments are properly scoped, which helps prevent misalignment and inefficiencies in the release process. Without this structured approach, the quality of the release cycle could degrade, leading to potential delays and governance issues.
