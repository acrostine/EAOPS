Compliance monitoring and reporting ()
The scheduled compliance monitoring cycle begins, or a significant model or project change event occurs, triggering the compliance monitoring and reporting process. This process encompasses the evaluation of architecture standards at the Control level, ensuring adherence to established architectural principles through a series of lifecycle phases including evidence gathering, findings assessment, and report compilation. It involves key stakeholders such as the architecture lead, EA practitioner, and review board, who make decisions based on the compliance status and any deviations noted during the assessment. The existence of this process is crucial for maintaining architectural integrity and ensuring that all projects align with the defined standards. Without it, there would be a risk of non-compliance, leading to potential architectural misalignment and inefficiencies.
