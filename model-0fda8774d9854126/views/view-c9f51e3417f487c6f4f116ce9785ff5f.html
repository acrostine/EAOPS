Review and approval coordination ()
The process is initiated when validated model changes are received from model development and semantic validation, along with cycle management feedback from stakeholder feedback collection. The Review and Approval Coordination process encompasses the entire lifecycle of reviewing and approving model changes, involving key stakeholders such as the architecture lead, review board, and stakeholders. Throughout its phases, the process ensures that all feedback is collected, consolidated, and assessed to reach a final decision on the approval of model changes, ultimately leading to the issuance of approved model changes. The Review and Approval Coordination process exists to provide structured governance over the release cycle, ensuring that all changes are thoroughly vetted and aligned with architectural standards. Without this process, the quality and compliance of model changes could degrade, leading to potential misalignment and inefficiencies in the release cycle.
