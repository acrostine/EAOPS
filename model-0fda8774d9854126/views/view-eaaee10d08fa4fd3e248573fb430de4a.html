Architecture adoption measurement ()
The scheduled measurement cycle is initiated by the architecture lead or a programme governance request, resulting in the production of architecture adoption metrics. The process encompasses defining measurement criteria, collecting adoption evidence, calculating adoption metrics, interpreting and contextualizing findings, and ultimately publishing metrics to the engagement channel. Key stakeholders, including EA practitioners, the architecture lead, and various stakeholders, are involved throughout the lifecycle to ensure that the metrics reflect the community's engagement and the effectiveness of the architecture. This process exists to deliver valuable insights into how well the architecture is being adopted within the community, facilitating informed decision-making and continuous improvement. Without this process, there would be a lack of clarity on adoption levels, leading to potential misalignment between stakeholder expectations and architectural outcomes.
