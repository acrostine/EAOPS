The Source-to-meta-model mapping process is initiated by a request or a scheduled event within the Repository Initialisation & Strategy capability. This process encompasses the assessment of the current state, the establishment of the EA strategic direction and roadmap, the transfer of information and seeding of the repository, and the design of the meta-model, ensuring that all elements align with the overarching architectural strategy. It exists to deliver value by ensuring that the foundational architecture repository is established in a way that supports strategic coherence and compliance with architectural standards, ultimately producing the Initial Architecture Blueprint for Architecture Standards & Compliance and leadership conversation output for Strategic Alignment & Leadership Engagement. <!-- SPEC --> **Lifecycle phases** - Current state assessment: evaluates the existing architecture and identifies gaps. - EA strategic direction and roadmap: defines the strategic objectives and the path to achieve them. - Information transfer and repository seeding: populates the repository with relevant data and artifacts. - Meta-model design: creates a structured representation of the architecture elements and their relationships.
