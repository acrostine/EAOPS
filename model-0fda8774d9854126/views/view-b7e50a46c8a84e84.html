The strategic intent discovery process is initiated by an EA initiation event, a strategic reset, or a leadership request aimed at surfacing architectural priorities, utilizing the current state report derived from the current state assessment. This process encompasses a series of activities that guide stakeholders through the preparation of dialogue, structured leadership conversations, extraction of vision and priority statements, identification of implicit architectural assumptions, and compilation of the raw strategic intent record. Key stakeholders, including the architecture lead, stakeholder, and EA practitioner, engage in this lifecycle to ensure that strategic initiatives are effectively aligned with organizational goals. The purpose of the strategic intent discovery process is to deliver value by providing a structured approach to translating architecture into strategic initiatives. Without this process, the organization risks misalignment between its strategic goals and architectural priorities, which could lead to ineffective decision-making and wasted resources.
