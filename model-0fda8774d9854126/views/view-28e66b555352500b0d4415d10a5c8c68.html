Stakeholder view design ()
The scheduled publication cycle begins or a stakeholder requests a new audience-specific view, initiating the stakeholder view design process. This process focuses on creating tailored views of architectural information that meet the needs of different stakeholders, progressing through several defined phases from identifying target audiences to publishing the designed views. It involves key decisions made by EA practitioners and architecture leads, ensuring that the final outputs align with the overarching architecture strategy and meet stakeholder expectations. The stakeholder view design process exists to enhance the communication of architectural information, ensuring that it is relevant and accessible to various audiences. Without this process, the dissemination of architectural insights would be inconsistent and potentially misaligned with stakeholder needs, leading to confusion and ineffective decision-making.
