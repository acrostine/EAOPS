Meta-model design ()
The process of Meta-model design is initiated by a request or a scheduled event within the Repository Initialisation & Strategy capability. This process involves the design and mapping of the meta-model that underpins the architecture repository, ensuring that the foundational elements align with the strategic direction of the enterprise architecture. Stakeholders engage in assessing the current state, defining the strategic direction, and transferring information to seed the repository, ultimately leading to the production of essential outputs that guide architectural standards and compliance. The purpose of the Meta-model design process is to establish a coherent framework that supports the architecture repository's functionality and strategic alignment. It encompasses the lifecycle from current state assessment through to the final design of the meta-model, making critical decisions about the structure and content of the repository. Without this process, the architecture repository would lack the necessary coherence and alignment with strategic objectives, leading to potential misalignment and inefficiencies in architectural governance. The value delivered by the Meta-model design process lies in its ability to create a structured and compliant architecture blueprint that serves as a foundation for future architectural endeavors. This process ensures that the architecture repository is not only established but also strategically aligned, facilitating effective communication and decision-making among leadership.
