Architecture adoption measurement ()
The scheduled measurement cycle is initiated by the architecture lead or a programme governance request, resulting in the production of architecture adoption metrics. The process encompasses defining measurement criteria, collecting adoption evidence, calculating adoption metrics, interpreting and contextualizing findings, and ultimately publishing metrics to the engagement channel. Key stakeholders, including EA practitioners, the architecture lead, and various stakeholders, are involved throughout the lifecycle to ensure that the metrics reflect the community's engagement and the effectiveness of the architecture. This process exists to deliver valuable insights into how well the architecture is being adopted within the community, facilitating informed decision-making and continuous improvement. Without this process, there would be a lack of clarity on adoption levels, leading to potential misalignment between stakeholder expectations and architectural outcomes. The primary output, architecture adoption metrics, is delivered to community engagement and dialogue, ensuring that stakeholders are informed and engaged. <!-- SPEC --> **Lifecycle phases** - define measurement criteria: establish the standards and metrics for assessing architecture adoption - collect adoption evidence: gather data and information on how the architecture is being utilized - calculate adoption metrics: analyze the collected evidence to derive meaningful metrics - interpret and contextualise findings: assess the metrics in the context of stakeholder needs and community engagement - publish metrics to engagement channel: disseminate the findings to relevant stakeholders and community members **Information objects** - architecture adoption metrics: produced by the calculation phase, arrives at the publish metrics phase, passed to community engagement and dialogue - compliance report: produced by the interpretation phase, arrives at the publish metrics phase, passed to community engagement and dialogue - quality report: produced by the interpretation phase, arrives at the publish metrics phase, passed to community engagement and dialogue - roadmap: produced by the interpretation phase, arrives at the publish metrics phase, passed to community engagement and dialogue
