Deviation and exception management ()
A solution design review feedback is received, identifying a non-conformance that requires exception consideration, which triggers the deviation and exception management process. This process involves several key activities, including receiving the deviation request, assessing the design review findings, evaluating the associated risks and business case, recording the disposition decision, and either closing the case or escalating it as necessary. Stakeholders such as the architecture lead, review board, and project team are involved throughout the lifecycle to ensure that all perspectives are considered in the decision-making process. The existence of this process is critical for maintaining compliance with established architectural principles and ensuring that any deviations are managed effectively. Without this process, there could be significant risks to architectural integrity, leading to potential misalignment with stakeholder needs and standards. The primary output of this process is the deviation record, which is communicated to Architecture Publishing for further action. <!-- SPEC --> **Lifecycle phases** - receive deviation request: the process begins with the submission of a deviation request based on design review feedback. - assess design review findings: the team evaluates the feedback to understand the nature of the non-conformance. - evaluate risk and business case: an assessment is conducted to weigh the risks and benefits of granting the exception. - record disposition decision: a decision is made regarding the deviation, which is documented accordingly. - close or escalate: the case is either closed if resolved or escalated for further review if necessary. **Information objects** - deviation record: produced by the assessment of design review findings, arrives at record disposition decision, passed to close or escalate - compliance report: produced by the architecture standards process, arrives at record disposition decision, passed to close or escalate - architecture principle: produced by the architecture standards process, arrives at assess design review findings, passed to evaluate risk and business case - architecture decision: produced by the record disposition decision, arrives at close or escalate, passed to Architecture Publishing - impact assessment: produced by the evaluate risk and business case, arrives at record disposition decision, passed to close or escalate
