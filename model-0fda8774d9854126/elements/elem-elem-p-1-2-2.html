Repository quality and hygiene ()
The scheduled hygiene interval is reached or updated models are submitted by model creation and curation, initiating the repository quality and hygiene process which results in a quality report. This process encompasses the assessment of the repository's structural integrity, classification of any identified issues, and the compilation of a comprehensive quality report. It involves stakeholders such as EA practitioners, architecture leads, and the repository system, ensuring that decisions made throughout the lifecycle contribute to the ongoing improvement of the repository. <!-- SPEC --> **Lifecycle phases** - trigger hygiene run: the process is initiated based on a scheduled interval or new model submissions - scan for structural issues: the repository is examined for any inconsistencies or problems - classify findings: identified issues are categorized based on severity and type - compile quality report: a report is generated summarizing the findings and classifications - publish report and close cycle: the quality report is shared with relevant stakeholders and the process concludes **Information objects** - architecture model: produced by model creation and curation, arrives at scan for structural issues, passed to classify findings - quality report: produced by compile quality report, arrives at publish report and close cycle, passed to repository evolution planning - compliance report: produced by scan for structural issues, arrives at classify findings, passed to compile quality report - published architecture feedback: produced by Architecture Publishing, arrives at compile quality report, passed to publish report and close cycle
