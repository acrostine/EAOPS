Impact analysis ()
The impact analysis process is initiated when the architecture scope and initial impact information are received from project intake and architecture scoping. This process focuses on evaluating the potential effects of proposed changes on existing architectural elements, guiding stakeholders through a structured assessment of risks and impacts. Key stakeholders, including EA practitioners, architecture leads, and project teams, collaborate to ensure that the analysis aligns with architectural standards and project requirements, ultimately leading to the creation of an impact analysis report. The process begins with the reception of scope and initial impact information, followed by the identification of affected model elements. Next, the magnitude and risk of changes are assessed, which informs the compilation of the impact analysis report. Finally, this report is delivered to the coaching and project team, ensuring that all relevant parties are equipped with the necessary insights to proceed effectively. Throughout this lifecycle, decisions are made based on the analysis of the architecture model, architecture decisions, architecture principles, and compliance reports. The impact analysis process exists to provide a thorough understanding of how changes will affect the architectural landscape, delivering value by mitigating risks and ensuring compliance with established principles. Without this process, projects may face unforeseen challenges and misalignments with architectural standards, leading to potential project failures. The primary output, the impact analysis report, is delivered to the delivery team coaching to facilitate informed decision-making. <!-- SPEC --> **Lifecycle phases** - receive scope and initial impact information: the process begins with the collection of relevant project scope and initial impact data. - identify affected model elements: the team identifies which elements of the architecture model will be impacted by the proposed changes. - assess magnitude and risk of changes: the potential impacts and associated risks of the changes are evaluated. - compile impact analysis report: findings are documented in a comprehensive report detailing the analysis. - deliver to coaching and project team: the final report is shared with the coaching and project team for further action. **Information objects** - impact assessment: produced by upstream source, arrives at phase identify affected model elements, passed to compile impact analysis report - architecture model: produced by upstream source, arrives at phase assess magnitude and risk of changes, passed to compile impact analysis report - architecture decision: produced by upstream source, arrives at phase assess magnitude and risk of changes, passed to compile impact analysis report - architecture principle: produced by upstream source, arrives at phase assess magnitude and risk of changes, passed to compile impact analysis report - compliance report: produced by upstream source, arrives at phase assess magnitude and risk of changes, passed to compile impact analysis report
