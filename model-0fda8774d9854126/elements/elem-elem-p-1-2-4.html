Repository evolution planning ()
A quality report received from repository quality and hygiene or the beginning of a scheduled planning cycle initiates the repository evolution planning process, resulting in the creation of a repository evolution roadmap. This process involves reviewing the quality report to identify structural gaps in the repository, prioritizing necessary changes, drafting a roadmap for implementation, and ultimately validating and publishing that roadmap. The existence of this process ensures that architectural knowledge is effectively managed and utilized, preventing degradation of repository quality and relevance; the primary output is the repository evolution roadmap, which is published to model creation and curation. <!-- SPEC --> **Lifecycle phases** - review quality report: assess the current state of the repository based on the quality report received. - identify structural gaps: determine areas where the repository does not meet required standards or needs improvement. - prioritise changes: evaluate and rank the identified changes based on their impact and urgency. - draft roadmap: create a detailed plan outlining the steps and timeline for implementing the prioritized changes. - validate and publish roadmap: ensure the roadmap meets stakeholder expectations and officially release it for execution. **Information objects** - quality report: produced by repository quality and hygiene, arrives at review quality report, passed to identify structural gaps. - repository evolution roadmap: produced by drafting roadmap, arrives at validate and publish roadmap, passed to model creation and curation. - architecture model: produced by model creation and curation, arrives at prioritise changes, passed to draft roadmap. - roadmap: produced by draft roadmap, arrives at validate and publish roadmap, passed to model creation and curation.
