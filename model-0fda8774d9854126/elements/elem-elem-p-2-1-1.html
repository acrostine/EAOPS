Architecture principles definition ()
The architecture principles definition process is initiated by a scheduled review cycle, the receipt of a compliance status report, or the submission of a standards update proposal. This process encompasses the evaluation and drafting of architectural principles, involving stakeholders such as the architecture lead and the review board, and progresses through several lifecycle phases from receiving proposals to publishing updated documentation. It exists to ensure that architectural standards are consistently defined and adhered to, delivering value by maintaining compliance and guiding solution design; without it, the integrity of architectural practices may degrade, ultimately producing architectural principles documentation that is delivered to solution design review. <!-- SPEC --> **Lifecycle phases** - receive compliance status and proposals: gather relevant compliance reports and proposals for review - review existing principles: assess current architectural principles for relevance and effectiveness - draft new or revised principles: create or modify principles based on the review findings - ratify with review board: present the drafted principles to the review board for approval - publish updated principles documentation: finalize and distribute the updated principles documentation **Information objects** - architecture principle: produced by drafting phase, arrives at ratification phase, passed to publishing phase - compliance report: produced by compliance monitoring, arrives at receive phase, passed to review phase - architecture decision: produced by ratification phase, arrives at publishing phase, passed to solution design review - standards update proposal: produced by Community & Stakeholder Engagement, arrives at receive phase, passed to review phase - architecture model: produced by architecture design, arrives at publishing phase, passed to solution design review
