Publication quality assurance ()
The process of publication quality assurance is initiated by the receipt of publication drafts from HTML publication management and release approval documentation from release authorization and publication. This process encompasses the evaluation of these drafts against established standards and guidelines, involving stakeholders such as EA practitioners, architecture leads, and the review board throughout its lifecycle. It ensures that all architectural information disseminated is of high quality and adheres to compliance requirements, as the absence of this process could lead to the release of substandard materials, ultimately degrading the integrity of architectural documentation; the primary output is a publication quality approval enabling a release or rejection report requiring rework. <!-- SPEC --> **Lifecycle phases** - receive publication drafts: the process begins with the collection of drafts and documentation for review. - check against release approval: the received drafts are compared to the release approval documentation to ensure compliance. - inspect content and formatting: a thorough examination of the content and formatting is conducted to identify any issues. - record findings: all observations and issues are documented for reference and further action. - approve or reject for release: based on the findings, the drafts are either approved for release or rejected, requiring rework. **Information objects** - stakeholder view: produced by upstream source, arrives at phase receive publication drafts, passed to downstream phase approve or reject for release. - release package: produced by upstream source, arrives at phase check against release approval, passed to downstream phase approve or reject for release. - quality report: produced by upstream source, arrives at phase record findings, passed to downstream phase approve or reject for release. - compliance report: produced by upstream source, arrives at phase check against release approval, passed to downstream phase approve or reject for release. - architecture decision: produced by upstream source, arrives at phase inspect content and formatting, passed to downstream phase approve or reject for release.
